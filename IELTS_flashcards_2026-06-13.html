IELTS Vocabulary Flashcards

Today's theme: Economy & Business  ·  8 words

Fiscal
adjective
Definition

Relating to government revenue, especially taxes and public spending. It can also describe a company's financial year.

Example

"The government introduced a series of fiscal measures to reduce the budget deficit and stimulate economic growth."

Collocations
fiscal policy fiscal year fiscal deficit
Idiom / Phrase

"Tighten the fiscal belt" — to reduce government spending to cut costs, often during an economic downturn.

💡 Memory tip: Think of a fishcal tank — the government controls what flows in (taxes) and out (spending).
Recession
noun
Definition

A period of significant economic decline marked by falling GDP for two or more consecutive quarters, often leading to higher unemployment.

Example

"The global recession of 2008 caused widespread job losses and triggered major reforms in financial regulation."

Collocations
economic recession plunge into recession recover from recession
Idiom / Phrase

"A double-dip recession" — when an economy briefly recovers but then contracts again before a full recovery takes hold.

💡 Memory tip: Re-cess-ion — the economy recedes, like water pulling back from a shore.
Austerity
noun
Definition

A set of government policies involving significant cuts to public expenditure and services in order to reduce a national deficit.

Example

"Years of austerity measures led to widespread protests as citizens bore the brunt of cuts to healthcare and education."

Collocations
austerity measures impose austerity era of austerity
Idiom / Phrase

"Tighten one's belt" — to spend less money and live more frugally, reflecting personal or national austerity.

💡 Memory tip: Austerity sounds like "austere" (harsh, bare) — a bare, stripped-down budget with no extras.
Entrepreneurship
noun
Definition

The process of designing, launching, and running a new business venture, typically involving financial risk in pursuit of innovation and profit.

Example

"Many universities now offer dedicated courses in entrepreneurship to equip graduates with the skills to build their own ventures."

Collocations
foster entrepreneurship social entrepreneurship spirit of entrepreneurship
Idiom / Phrase

"Take the plunge" — to make a bold decision to start a business despite uncertainty or risk.

💡 Memory tip: "Entre" = enter (French). An entrepreneur enters the market where others haven't dared.
Commodity
noun
Definition

A raw material or primary agricultural product that can be bought and sold, such as copper, oil, or wheat; more broadly, any good or service with uniform quality traded in bulk.

Example

"Fluctuations in global commodity prices can significantly impact the economies of developing nations that rely on exports."

Collocations
commodity prices commodity market scarce commodity
Idiom / Phrase

"Time is a scarce commodity" — used to emphasise that time is valuable and in limited supply, just like a traded resource.

💡 Memory tip: A common good for everyone — "commod" shares its root with "common" (Latin: commodus).
Diversify
verb
Definition

To enlarge or vary a company's range of products, investments, or operations in order to reduce risk and open new revenue streams.

Example

"To remain competitive, the firm decided to diversify its portfolio by expanding into renewable energy and digital services."

Collocations
diversify a portfolio diversify into new markets diversify revenue streams
Idiom / Phrase

"Don't put all your eggs in one basket" — the classic justification for diversifying investments or business strategies.

💡 Memory tip: Diverse + -ify = to make diverse. Picture a basket filled with many different eggs — safe no matter which drops.
Procurement
noun
Definition

The formal process by which organisations obtain goods, services, or works from an external source, typically through competitive tendering or negotiated contracts.

Example

"The hospital implemented a centralised procurement system to reduce costs and ensure the consistent supply of medical equipment."

Collocations
public procurement procurement process strategic procurement
Idiom / Phrase

"Source at the best price" — the informal guiding principle behind procurement: obtain what you need at the most favourable terms.

💡 Memory tip: Pro-cure = to professionally cure (solve) a supply need. Procurement is the cure for a shortage.
Liquidate
verb
Definition

To wind up a business by converting its assets into cash in order to pay creditors; more generally, to settle a debt or close out an investment position.

Example

"When the company failed to service its debts, creditors sought a court order to liquidate its remaining assets."

Collocations
liquidate assets forced liquidation liquidate a position
Idiom / Phrase

"Turn into cash" — to liquidate something is quite literally to make it liquid (i.e., cash) rather than a fixed asset.

💡 Memory tip: Liquid-ate — assets become liquid (cash) like ice melting into water. Solid wealth → flowing money.
