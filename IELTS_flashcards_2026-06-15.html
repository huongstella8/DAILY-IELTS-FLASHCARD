Mitigation
noun
📖 Definition
The action of reducing the severity, seriousness, or painfulness of something. In environmental contexts, it refers to efforts to reduce or prevent the emission of greenhouse gases.
✏️ Example
"Governments worldwide are investing in renewable energy as a key mitigation strategy to limit the effects of global warming."
🔗 Collocations
climate mitigation mitigation measures risk mitigation
💬 Idiom / Fixed Phrase
"nip it in the bud" — to stop something harmful before it develops further, much like mitigation aims to prevent climate damage early on.
💡 Memory Tip
Think: mitigate = make mighty problems mighty smaller. The "mit-" root means "to send away" — you're sending the problem away.
Biodiversity
noun
📖 Definition
The variety of plant and animal life in a particular habitat or on Earth as a whole. High biodiversity is considered essential for ecosystem stability and resilience.
✏️ Example
"Deforestation poses a severe threat to biodiversity, as it destroys the habitats of countless species that have yet to be discovered by science."
🔗 Collocations
protect biodiversity loss of biodiversity biodiversity hotspot
💬 Idiom / Fixed Phrase
"the web of life" — a phrase capturing how all living species are interconnected, much as biodiversity describes the richness of those connections.
💡 Memory Tip
Bio = life, diversity = variety. Picture a forest bursting with every colour of creature — that riot of variety is biodiversity.
Desertification
noun
📖 Definition
The process by which fertile land becomes desert, typically as a result of drought, deforestation, or inappropriate agriculture. It is one of the major environmental challenges facing arid regions.
✏️ Example
"The Sahel region of Africa is particularly vulnerable to desertification, which threatens food security for millions of people."
🔗 Collocations
combat desertification prevent desertification land degradation
💬 Idiom / Fixed Phrase
"turn to dust" — to deteriorate completely, mirroring the literal transformation of fertile land into barren desert.
💡 Memory Tip
It's right in the word: desert + fication = the process of becoming a desert. Visualise green land slowly turning to sand.
Renewable
adjective
📖 Definition
Describing a natural resource or energy source that is not depleted when used, because it is naturally replenished on a human timescale, such as wind, solar, or hydropower.
✏️ Example
"A rapid transition to renewable energy sources is widely regarded as indispensable to meeting international climate targets."
🔗 Collocations
renewable energy renewable resources renewable sector
💬 Idiom / Fixed Phrase
"harness the power of nature" — to capture and use natural forces productively, exactly what renewable energy does with wind and sunlight.
💡 Memory Tip
Re-new-able — something that renews itself. The sun rises every morning; it never runs out. That renewal is the key idea.
Carbon Footprint
noun phrase
📖 Definition
The total amount of greenhouse gases, particularly carbon dioxide, that are generated by an individual, organisation, event, or product, expressed as equivalent tonnes of CO₂.
✏️ Example
"Many corporations now publish annual sustainability reports detailing the steps they are taking to reduce their carbon footprint."
🔗 Collocations
reduce carbon footprint calculate carbon footprint offset emissions
💬 Idiom / Fixed Phrase
"leave a mark on the planet" — the invisible but measurable trace every person leaves on Earth's climate, the essence of a carbon footprint.
💡 Memory Tip
Imagine walking on fresh concrete — you leave a footprint. Every activity "stamps" carbon into the atmosphere. The bigger the activity, the bigger the stamp.
Deforestation
noun
📖 Definition
The large-scale clearing or thinning of forests by humans, often to make land available for farming or urban development. It is a leading driver of habitat loss and CO₂ release.
✏️ Example
"Rampant deforestation in tropical regions has significantly reduced the Earth's capacity to absorb atmospheric carbon dioxide."
🔗 Collocations
rampant deforestation halt deforestation illegal logging
💬 Idiom / Fixed Phrase
"can't see the wood for the trees" — ironically, deforestation means we may soon lack both the wood and the trees, having lost the forest ecosystem entirely.
💡 Memory Tip
De-forest-ation: the prefix "de-" means removal. Removing the forest. Picture a green map with huge patches being erased.
Ecosystem
noun
📖 Definition
A biological community of interacting organisms and their physical environment. Ecosystems can range in scale from a pond to the entire biosphere and provide services essential to human life.
✏️ Example
"Coral reefs are among the most biologically diverse ecosystems on Earth, yet they are critically threatened by rising ocean temperatures."
🔗 Collocations
fragile ecosystem restore ecosystems ecosystem services
💬 Idiom / Fixed Phrase
"a delicate balance" — ecosystems depend on equilibrium; tip one factor and the whole system can collapse, like a house of cards.
💡 Memory Tip
Eco = home (Greek: oikos) + system. An ecosystem is nature's household — every member has a role, and the household only runs when everyone pulls their weight.
Sustainable
adjective
📖 Definition
Able to be maintained at a certain rate or level without depleting natural resources or causing severe ecological damage; meeting the needs of the present without compromising future generations.
✏️ Example
"Transitioning to sustainable agricultural practices is essential if we are to feed a growing global population while preserving soil quality."
🔗 Collocations
sustainable development sustainable practices environmentally sustainable
💬 Idiom / Fixed Phrase
"don't kill the goose that lays the golden egg" — don't destroy the resource that provides long-term benefit; sustainability is exactly this principle applied to the planet.
💡 Memory Tip
Sustain = to hold up. Sustainable means the planet can keep holding everything up without collapsing. Think of Atlas holding the world — sustainably.
