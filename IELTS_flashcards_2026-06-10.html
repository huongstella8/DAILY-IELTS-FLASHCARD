IELTS Vocabulary

Technology & Innovation

Wednesday, 10 June 2026  ·  8 flashcards

algorithm noun
Definition A set of rules or instructions followed by a computer to solve a problem or complete a task. Algorithms form the backbone of all software and digital decision-making systems.
Example sentence Social media platforms use sophisticated algorithms to curate content and maximise user engagement.
Collocations
design an algorithm machine-learning algorithm run an algorithm
Idiom / Fixed phrase "black-box algorithm" — an algorithm whose internal workings are opaque or unexplainable to outside observers.
Memory tip Think of Al Gore + rhythm: a rhythm is a pattern of steps — so an algorithm is a pattern of logical steps.
automation noun
Definition The use of technology to perform tasks with minimal human intervention, often replacing manual labour. Automation is a defining feature of the modern industrial and digital economy.
Example sentence The widespread automation of manufacturing processes has significantly boosted productivity while simultaneously displacing low-skilled workers.
Collocations
drive automation industrial automation accelerate automation
Idiom / Fixed phrase "lights-out factory" — a fully automated facility that can operate in complete darkness because no human workers are present.
Memory tip Auto = self; mation ~ motion. A self-moving (self-operating) process = automation.
disruptive adjective
Definition Causing radical change by introducing a new product, service, or technology that displaces established competitors. In business contexts, "disruptive" is largely positive.
Example sentence Ride-hailing apps proved to be disruptive innovations that fundamentally transformed the traditional taxi industry.
Collocations
disruptive technology disruptive innovation disruptive force
Idiom / Fixed phrase "shake up the market" — to disrupt an established industry by introducing something radically new.
Memory tip Picture a smooth road (rupt = break) being disconnected — the technology breaks apart what already existed.
proliferation noun
Definition A rapid and widespread increase in the number or spread of something. In technology contexts, it often describes the fast expansion of devices, platforms, or digital tools.
Example sentence The proliferation of smartphones in developing nations has democratised access to information and financial services.
Collocations
rapid proliferation the proliferation of devices curb proliferation
Idiom / Fixed phrase "spread like wildfire" — to proliferate extremely quickly and in an uncontrolled manner.
Memory tip Prolific means producing a lot → proliferation is the explosion of prolific production.
augmented adjective
Definition Enhanced or supplemented, especially by the addition of digital information overlaid on the real world. Widely used in "augmented reality" (AR) contexts.
Example sentence Augmented reality applications are increasingly being deployed in surgery, enabling physicians to visualise anatomical data in real time.
Collocations
augmented reality augmented intelligence augmented workforce
Idiom / Fixed phrase "layer on top of" — to add digital information augmented over the physical world, as in AR.
Memory tip Augment sounds like august (great, majestic) — augmented = made greater by adding to it.
obsolescence noun
Definition The process of becoming outdated or no longer useful, typically because something newer or more efficient has replaced it. Common in discussions of rapid technological change.
Example sentence Planned obsolescence — designing products to fail after a fixed period — has been widely criticised as environmentally irresponsible.
Collocations
planned obsolescence technological obsolescence risk obsolescence
Idiom / Fixed phrase "past its sell-by date" — so outdated as to be useless; obsolete.
Memory tip Obsolete → obsolescence. The -escence ending signals a process of becoming (like adolescence = becoming an adult).
scalability noun
Definition The capacity of a system, network, or process to handle a growing amount of work or to be expanded to accommodate growth without losing performance.
Example sentence Cloud computing is prized for its scalability, allowing businesses to expand their digital infrastructure on demand.
Collocations
ensure scalability horizontal scalability limit scalability
Idiom / Fixed phrase "grow without growing pains" — to scale up smoothly, an expression of good scalability.
Memory tip Imagine a scale (weighing scale) that can keep expanding its range — the ability to scale up = scalability.
convergence noun
Definition The merging of distinct technologies, industries, or systems into a unified whole. Technological convergence describes how formerly separate devices and services combine into single platforms.
Example sentence The convergence of telecommunications, computing, and media has fundamentally altered how societies produce and consume content.
Collocations
technological convergence digital convergence a point of convergence
Idiom / Fixed phrase "two roads meet" — a vivid image for convergence: separate paths coming together at a single point.
Memory tip Con- (together) + verge (edge/direction) → moving towards the same edge. Rivers converge; so do technologies.
