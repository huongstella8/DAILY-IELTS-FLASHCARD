Society & Culture

Friday, June 12, 2026
8 Flashcards
assimilate verb
Definition

To absorb and integrate individuals or groups into a wider society or culture, often adopting its customs and values.

Example Sentence

Immigrants who struggle to assimilate into the host culture often face social isolation and limited economic opportunities.

Collocations
  • assimilate into mainstream society
  • assimilate cultural norms
  • fully / rapidly assimilate
Idiom / Fixed Phrase

blend in — to assimilate so thoroughly that one is indistinguishable from the surrounding group: "She quickly blended in with the local community."

Memory Tip

Think of a sponge (similar) that absorbs — assi-MILATE sounds like "a similar mate," someone who becomes like those around them.

pluralism noun
Definition

A condition or system in which multiple distinct ethnic, cultural, or political groups coexist within a single society, each retaining its own identity.

Example Sentence

Cultural pluralism is increasingly viewed as a social asset, fostering creativity and tolerance in modern democracies.

Collocations
  • cultural / religious pluralism
  • embrace pluralism
  • a model of pluralism
Idiom / Fixed Phrase

a mosaic society — a society composed of distinct cultural pieces that together form a vibrant whole, often used as a metaphor for pluralism.

Memory Tip

Plural = many. Pluralism = a society with many different groups living side by side. Imagine a colourful mosaic tile made of plural pieces.

cohesion noun
Definition

The quality of forming a united whole; the tendency of members of a group or society to stick together and remain unified.

Example Sentence

Governments often invest in community programmes to strengthen social cohesion and reduce inter-group tensions.

Collocations
  • social / community cohesion
  • promote / undermine cohesion
  • a lack of cohesion
Idiom / Fixed Phrase

the glue that holds society together — cohesion is often described as the "glue" binding a community: "Shared values are the glue that holds society together."

Memory Tip

Cohere = stick together. Cohesion is the stickiness of society. Picture social groups held together with invisible glue.

marginalize verb
Definition

To treat a person or group as socially insignificant or peripheral, excluding them from power, influence, or mainstream participation.

Example Sentence

Minority communities have historically been marginalized in political decision-making processes, limiting their access to public resources.

Collocations
  • marginalized groups / communities
  • further marginalize the poor
  • feel / become marginalized
Idiom / Fixed Phrase

pushed to the margins — a vivid phrase meaning excluded from the centre of society: "These workers have been pushed to the margins of the economy."

Memory Tip

Think of a margin on a page — it's the blank edge, ignored. To marginalize = to push someone to the margin, away from the main text of society.

heritage noun
Definition

The traditions, achievements, and values passed down through generations within a culture or nation; the legacy of a society's past.

Example Sentence

Preserving intangible cultural heritage, such as oral traditions and performing arts, is a priority for UNESCO and member states alike.

Collocations
  • cultural / national heritage
  • preserve / protect heritage
  • a UNESCO World Heritage Site
Idiom / Fixed Phrase

roots and branches — heritage represents the roots (origins) of a culture from which future generations (branches) grow: "Understanding one's roots is fundamental to cultural identity."

Memory Tip

Inherit → heritage. Both come from Latin heres (heir). Heritage is simply what you inherit from the past — a cultural gift from your ancestors.

demographic noun / adj
Definition

Relating to the structure, distribution, and characteristics of a population; as a noun, a particular sector of a population defined by age, gender, income, etc.

Example Sentence

Rapid ageing is the most significant demographic shift facing industrialised nations, placing considerable strain on pension and healthcare systems.

Collocations
  • demographic shift / change / trend
  • ageing demographic
  • target a specific demographic
Idiom / Fixed Phrase

a greying population — a common metaphor for an ageing demographic: "A greying population will reshape social spending priorities over the next decade."

Memory Tip

Demo = people (Greek demos) + graphic = written/measured. A demographic is a measured picture of people — like a statistical portrait of society.

norm noun
Definition

An expected or standard pattern of behaviour within a particular society or social group; an unwritten rule governing conduct.

Example Sentence

Social norms surrounding gender roles have evolved considerably in many societies, though significant variation persists across cultures.

Collocations
  • social / cultural norms
  • conform to / deviate from norms
  • challenge established norms
Idiom / Fixed Phrase

par for the course — meaning something is the norm or expected standard: "Long working hours have become par for the course in many industries."

Memory Tip

Normal → norm. A norm is the normal way of behaving — what "everyone does." It's the invisible rulebook society hands you at birth.

disparity noun
Definition

A great difference or inequality between two or more things, especially in opportunities, outcomes, or treatment within a society.

Example Sentence

Widening income disparity between the wealthy elite and the working poor has fuelled social unrest in several developed nations.

Collocations
  • income / racial / gender disparity
  • glaring / stark disparity
  • address / narrow the disparity
Idiom / Fixed Phrase

the wealth gap — a widely used phrase capturing economic disparity: "Policymakers have struggled to close the wealth gap between urban and rural communities."

Memory Tip

Dis (apart) + par (equal, as in "par" in golf) = not equal, pulled apart. Disparity is when two things are not at the same par — one is far above the other.
