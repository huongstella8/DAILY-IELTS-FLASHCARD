IELTS Vocabulary Flashcards

🩺 Health & Medicine
ailment
noun
Definition
A minor illness or physical complaint, typically one that is recurring or persistent rather than life-threatening.
Example
Respiratory ailments are increasingly common in urban areas with high levels of air pollution.
Collocations
common ailment · chronic ailment · treat an ailment
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
cure what ails you — to fix whatever is wrong with you, physically or metaphorically.
Memory Tip
AILment — if you ail, you suffer from an ailment. Keep it simple!
antibiotic
noun / adjective
Definition
A medicine that kills or inhibits the growth of bacteria, used to treat bacterial infections.
Example
The overuse of antibiotics in agriculture has accelerated the emergence of drug-resistant bacterial strains.
Collocations
prescribe antibiotics · antibiotic resistance · broad-spectrum antibiotic
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
antibiotic apocalypse — a public health crisis scenario in which no antibiotics remain effective against superbugs.
Memory Tip
ANTI + BIOTIC = against life (bacterial life). It's a weapon against microbes.
benign
adjective
Definition
(Of a disease or tumour) not harmful or cancerous; also used broadly to mean gentle or not threatening.
Example
The biopsy confirmed that the growth was benign, greatly relieving the patient's anxiety.
Collocations
benign tumour · benign condition · clinically benign
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
benign neglect — a policy of deliberately not intervening, used in both medicine and politics.
Memory Tip
BENE (Latin = good) → benign = "good-natured." Contrast with malignant (bad).
chronic
adjective
Definition
Persisting for a long time or recurring frequently; describes a disease or condition that is long-lasting rather than acute.
Example
Chronic back pain is one of the leading causes of disability and lost productivity worldwide.
Collocations
chronic disease · chronic pain · chronically ill
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
chronic condition — standard medical phrase for any long-term, ongoing health issue.
Memory Tip
CHRONos (Greek = time) → chronic = lasting a long time. Think "chronological."
cognition
noun
Definition
The mental processes of acquiring knowledge and understanding through thought, experience, and the senses.
Example
Research has shown that regular aerobic exercise can significantly improve cognition in older adults.
Collocations
impair cognition · social cognition · cognitive decline
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
cognitive dissonance — the mental discomfort of holding two conflicting beliefs simultaneously.
Memory Tip
COGITO ergo sum — Descartes: "I think, therefore I am." Cognition = thinking.
contagious
adjective
Definition
(Of a disease) able to spread from one person to another through direct or indirect contact.
Example
Measles is highly contagious and can spread rapidly through unvaccinated populations.
Collocations
highly contagious · contagious disease · contagious period
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
contagious enthusiasm — enthusiasm so strong that it spreads to others (metaphorical use).
Memory Tip
CONTACT → contagious. It spreads through contact. The words share the same Latin root.
diagnosis
noun
Definition
The identification of the nature of an illness or other problem by examination and analysis of its signs and symptoms.
Example
An early diagnosis of Type 2 diabetes can allow for lifestyle interventions that prevent serious complications.
Collocations
make a diagnosis · early diagnosis · definitive diagnosis
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
differential diagnosis — the process of distinguishing between diseases with similar symptoms.
Memory Tip
DIA (through) + GNOSIS (knowledge) = knowing something thoroughly. Doctors "know through" the symptoms.
epidemic
noun / adjective
Definition
A widespread occurrence of a disease in a community or region at a particular time, affecting more people than normally expected.
Example
The obesity epidemic poses one of the greatest public health challenges of the twenty-first century.
Collocations
contain an epidemic · epidemic proportions · drug epidemic
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
reach epidemic proportions — used when any problem (not just disease) has spread alarmingly widely.
Memory Tip
EPI (upon) + DEMOS (people) = falling upon the people. Compare pandemic = across ALL people.
