Prognosis
noun
📖 Definition
A medical opinion about the likely course or outcome of a disease or condition.
✍️ Example
"Early detection of cancer significantly improves the patient's prognosis, often enabling complete recovery."
🔗 Collocations
poor prognosisfavourable prognosisgive a prognosis
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"The writing is on the wall" — used when the prognosis is clearly negative, e.g., "For untreated patients, the writing was on the wall."
💡 Memory Tip
Pro- (before) + gnosis (knowledge) → "knowledge before" the illness unfolds. Think: a fortune teller's crystal ball for doctors.
Palliative
adjective / noun
📖 Definition
Relieving the symptoms of a disease or condition without treating the underlying cause; relating to end-of-life comfort care.
✍️ Example
"Palliative care focuses on alleviating pain and improving quality of life for terminally ill patients."
🔗 Collocations
palliative carepalliative treatmentpalliative measures
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"A band-aid solution" — much like palliative treatment, it addresses the surface without curing the root problem.
💡 Memory Tip
From Latin palliare — to cloak or cover. Palliative care "covers" the pain rather than removing the disease. Picture draping a warm blanket over a patient.
Prevalence
noun
📖 Definition
The proportion of a population found to have a particular condition or disease at a specific time.
✍️ Example
"The prevalence of type 2 diabetes has risen sharply in urban populations over the past two decades."
🔗 Collocations
high prevalenceprevalence of diseasegrowing prevalence
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"Widespread" — used interchangeably in academic texts, e.g., "Obesity has become widespread in developed nations."
💡 Memory Tip
Preval- sounds like "prevail." When a disease has high prevalence, it is prevailing — winning in numbers — across the population.
Pathogen
noun
📖 Definition
A microorganism — such as a bacterium, virus, or fungus — that causes disease in a host.
✍️ Example
"Understanding how a pathogen evades the immune system is critical to developing effective vaccines."
🔗 Collocations
airborne pathogentransmit a pathogendrug-resistant pathogen
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"Public enemy number one" — journalists sometimes use this phrase to describe a particularly dangerous pathogen during an outbreak.
💡 Memory Tip
Patho- (suffering) + -gen (to generate). A pathogen generates suffering. Connect it to "pathetic" — something pitifully causing harm.
Sedentary
adjective
📖 Definition
Characterised by prolonged sitting and insufficient physical activity; describing a largely inactive lifestyle.
✍️ Example
"A sedentary lifestyle is now recognised as an independent risk factor for cardiovascular disease, regardless of diet."
🔗 Collocations
sedentary lifestylesedentary behavioursedentary occupation
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"Couch potato" — colloquial phrase for someone with a sedentary lifestyle; useful to contrast in IELTS essays.
💡 Memory Tip
Think of sediment — particles that settle and sit still at the bottom. A sedentary person similarly "settles" without movement.
Holistic
adjective
📖 Definition
Treating or considering the whole person — physical, mental, social, and environmental factors — rather than just symptoms alone.
✍️ Example
"A holistic approach to healthcare integrates mental health support with conventional medical treatment."
🔗 Collocations
holistic approachholistic medicineholistic well-being
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"See the big picture" — a holistic practitioner always sees the big picture of a patient's life, not just the presenting symptom.
💡 Memory Tip
Hol- comes from Greek holos = whole. Holistic = treating the whole. Remember: "It's a whole lot more than just the disease."
Malnutrition
noun
📖 Definition
A condition resulting from an inadequate, excess, or imbalanced intake of nutrients, causing measurable adverse health effects.
✍️ Example
"Malnutrition in early childhood can cause irreversible developmental delays that persist into adulthood."
🔗 Collocations
combat malnutritionsuffer from malnutritionchronic malnutrition
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"Going without" — used empathetically in essays to describe populations experiencing malnutrition due to poverty or conflict.
💡 Memory Tip
Mal- (bad) + nutrition. Simple equation: bad nutrition = malnutrition. Contrast with "bon" (good) in French — bon vs. mal — good food vs. bad intake.
Immunocompromised
adjective
📖 Definition
Having a weakened or impaired immune system, making a person more vulnerable to infections and disease.
✍️ Example
"Immunocompromised patients, such as those undergoing chemotherapy, must take additional precautions to avoid exposure to infectious agents."
🔗 Collocations
immunocompromised patientseverely immunocompromisedprotect the immunocompromised
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"Defences are down" — when someone's immune system is compromised, their body's natural defences are down, leaving them exposed.
💡 Memory Tip
Break it down: immuno (immune system) + compromised (weakened/damaged). Picture an army (immune system) that has been "compromised" — half its soldiers are gone.
