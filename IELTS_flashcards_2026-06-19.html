IELTS Vocabulary – Society & Culture

8 flashcards · Academic Word List focus

assimilation
noun
Definition
The process by which a person or group adopts the culture, language, and customs of a dominant society, often losing their original identity in the process.
Example Sentence
"Critics argue that forced assimilation policies erode the cultural heritage of minority communities and undermine social diversity."
Collocations
cultural assimilation rapid assimilation forced assimilation
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
🗣 melt into the mainstream — to be absorbed so thoroughly into a dominant culture that one's original background becomes invisible.
Memory Tip
💡 Think of assimilation as becoming "similar" — newcomers become similar to the host culture. Assim-il-ation → similar.
bureaucracy
noun
Definition
A system of government or administration characterised by complex rules, rigid hierarchies, and an excessive number of procedures that can slow down decision-making.
Example Sentence
"Entrepreneurs frequently cite excessive bureaucracy as a significant barrier to starting a business in developing economies."
Collocations
cut through bureaucracy bureaucratic red tape government bureaucracy
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
🗣 red tape — excessive official rules and paperwork that delay or prevent action; closely tied to bureaucracy.
Memory Tip
💡 Bureau (French for "office") + -cracy (rule). Picture a dusty office buried under piles of paper — that's bureaucracy.
cohesion
noun
Definition
The quality of forming a unified whole; in social contexts, it refers to the bonds that hold communities or groups together through shared values and mutual trust.
Example Sentence
"Rising income inequality has been shown to erode social cohesion, making communities more fragmented and less trusting."
Collocations
social cohesion community cohesion promote cohesion
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
🗣 hold together at the seams — to barely maintain unity; a society with weak cohesion is "coming apart at the seams."
Memory Tip
💡 Co- (together) + here (to stick). Cohesion = things sticking together. Think of glue holding a community in place.
demographics
noun (plural)
Definition
Statistical data relating to the characteristics of a population, such as age, gender, income, ethnicity, and education level, used to understand social trends.
Example Sentence
"Shifting demographics, including an ageing population and declining birth rates, are placing unprecedented strain on pension systems worldwide."
Collocations
changing demographics demographic shift target demographic
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
🗣 the numbers tell a different story — used when demographic data contradicts popular assumptions about a society.
Memory Tip
💡 Demo- (people, as in democracy) + -graphics (writing/data). Demographics = data written about the people.
diaspora
noun
Definition
The dispersion of a people from their original homeland, or the communities formed by such people in the countries where they have settled.
Example Sentence
"The Indian diaspora, spread across more than 100 countries, plays a vital role in transferring remittances back to their homeland."
Collocations
diaspora community global diaspora maintain diaspora ties
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
🗣 scattered to the four winds — describing people or groups dispersed in all directions, much like a diaspora.
Memory Tip
💡 From Greek diaspeirein: dia- (across) + speirein (to sow/scatter). Picture seeds scattered across a field — each seed is a diaspora community.
discrimination
noun
Definition
The unjust or prejudicial treatment of individuals or groups based on characteristics such as race, gender, age, religion, or disability.
Example Sentence
"Anti-discrimination legislation has made significant strides in protecting workers from unfair treatment in the workplace."
Collocations
racial discrimination face discrimination combat discrimination
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
🗣 two-tier system — a structure where one group receives better treatment than another, reflecting systemic discrimination.
Memory Tip
💡 To discriminate literally means "to distinguish between." When applied unfairly to people, it becomes harmful. Remember: "dis-" (apart) + "criminate" (separate).
diversity
noun
Definition
The presence and inclusion of a wide range of different people, cultures, backgrounds, ideas, or perspectives within a group, organisation, or society.
Example Sentence
"Research consistently shows that workplace diversity fosters creativity and leads to better problem-solving outcomes across industries."
Collocations
cultural diversity embrace diversity diversity and inclusion
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
🗣 a rich tapestry — used to describe a society or group whose diversity creates something more beautiful and complex than any single thread alone.
Memory Tip
💡 From Latin diversus = turned in different directions. Picture a compass with arrows pointing everywhere — that's diversity of direction and thought.
emigration
noun
Definition
The act of leaving one's own country to settle permanently in another; the process of moving away from a home nation, typically for economic, political, or social reasons.
Example Sentence
"Mass emigration of skilled professionals, often called 'brain drain', poses a serious challenge to the development of many low-income nations."
Collocations
mass emigration emigration wave economic emigration
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
🗣 seek greener pastures — to leave one's home in search of better opportunities elsewhere, a key driver of emigration.
Memory Tip
💡 E- (out) + migrate. You EXIT your country. Compare with immigration (you enter INTO a new one). "E = Exit, I = Into."
