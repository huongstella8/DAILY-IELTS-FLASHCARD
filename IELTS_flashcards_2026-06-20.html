Economy & Business

8 IELTS Academic Vocabulary Flashcards

fiscal
adjective
Definition
Relating to government revenue, especially taxes; or more broadly, relating to financial matters.
Example
"The government introduced a new fiscal policy to reduce the national debt through targeted spending cuts."
Collocations
fiscal policy fiscal year fiscal deficit
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
fiscal cliff — a sudden sharp reduction in government spending that can trigger economic recession.
Memory Tip
Think of fiscal → fiscus (Latin for "treasury basket"). The government's money basket.
recession
noun
Definition
A period of temporary economic decline marked by reduced trade and industrial activity, typically defined as two consecutive quarters of negative GDP growth.
Example
"The global recession of 2008 led to widespread unemployment and a significant contraction in international trade."
Collocations
economic recession fall into recession recession-hit
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
in the teeth of a recession — operating under extremely difficult economic conditions.
Memory Tip
Re-cession = the economy recedes (moves backward). Picture a tide pulling back from the shore.
inflation
noun
Definition
A general and sustained increase in prices across an economy, resulting in a decrease in the purchasing power of money.
Example
"Rising inflation eroded household savings and prompted central banks to raise interest rates sharply."
Collocations
curb inflation inflation rate rampant inflation
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
inflation eats into savings — describes how rising prices gradually reduce the real value of money saved.
Memory Tip
Like inflating a balloon — prices keep expanding until something pops.
austerity
noun
Definition
Government economic policies that reduce spending or increase taxes to reduce budget deficits, often at the cost of public services.
Example
"Years of austerity measures led to significant cuts in healthcare and education budgets, sparking widespread public protests."
Collocations
austerity measures impose austerity era of austerity
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
tighten one's belt — to spend less money; the individual equivalent of government austerity.
Memory Tip
Austerity sounds like austere (harsh, severe). A harsh diet for the government budget.
subsidy
noun
Definition
A sum of money granted by the government or a public body to assist an industry or business so that the price of a commodity or service may remain low or competitive.
Example
"Agricultural subsidies have been criticised for distorting free trade and disadvantaging farmers in developing nations."
Collocations
government subsidy withdraw a subsidy subsidy scheme
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
level the playing field — subsidies are often debated as either levelling or distorting the competitive playing field.
Memory Tip
Subsidy = sits under the price — it's the financial support propping things up from below.
entrepreneur
noun
Definition
A person who organises and operates a business or businesses, taking on financial risk in the hope of generating profit and innovation.
Example
"Successful entrepreneurs not only generate personal wealth but also create jobs and drive technological advancement within the economy."
Collocations
serial entrepreneur entrepreneurial spirit social entrepreneur
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
take the plunge — to commit to starting a business despite uncertainty, as entrepreneurs do.
Memory Tip
From French entreprendre = "to undertake." An entrepreneur under-takes ventures others won't touch.
revenue
noun
Definition
The income generated by a business or government before expenses are deducted; also called "the top line" in financial reporting.
Example
"The company's annual revenue grew by 18%, driven largely by expanding into emerging markets in Southeast Asia."
Collocations
generate revenue tax revenue revenue stream
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
top line vs bottom line — revenue is the "top line" (total income); profit is the "bottom line" (what remains after costs).
Memory Tip
Revenue = money that re-venes (comes back in). Think of it as a river flowing into the company.
dividend
noun
Definition
A sum of money paid regularly by a company to its shareholders out of profits; also used figuratively to mean a benefit from an action or investment.
Example
"Investors were reassured when the board announced it would maintain its quarterly dividend despite a challenging financial year."
Collocations
pay a dividend dividend yield peace dividend
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
pay dividends — (figurative) to produce benefits or rewards later as a result of earlier efforts: "Hard work pays dividends."
Memory Tip
Dividend — profits are divided among shareholders. The "nd" ending makes it the thing being divided out.
