🌿 Science & Nature
Biodiversity
noun
📖 Definition
The variety of plant and animal life in a particular habitat or on Earth as a whole, considered in terms of the number of different species present.
✏️ Example
"Deforestation poses one of the gravest threats to global biodiversity, eliminating entire species before they are even discovered."
🔗 Collocations
preserve biodiversity rich in biodiversity biodiversity hotspot
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"The web of life" — used to describe how all species are interconnected, underlining why biodiversity loss is so devastating.
💡 Memory Tip
Bio (life) + diverse (varied) = the variety of life. Think of a rainforest bursting with different coloured creatures.
Phenomenon
noun
📖 Definition
A fact, occurrence, or circumstance observed or observable in the natural world, especially one whose cause or explanation is in question.
✏️ Example
"The aurora borealis is a natural phenomenon caused by charged solar particles colliding with atmospheric gases."
🔗 Collocations
natural phenomenon widespread phenomenon explain a phenomenon
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"A force of nature" — describes an overwhelming natural phenomenon or an exceptionally dynamic person.
💡 Memory Tip
Plural is phenomena (not phenomenons!). Think of it as something that makes you say "phenomenal!" because it's so striking.
Hypothesis
noun
📖 Definition
A proposed explanation for an observation or phenomenon that can be tested through further investigation and experimentation.
✏️ Example
"The researchers formulated a hypothesis that elevated CO₂ levels accelerate coral bleaching, then designed controlled experiments to test it."
🔗 Collocations
formulate a hypothesis test a hypothesis confirm / refute a hypothesis
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"Working hypothesis" — a provisional assumption accepted for the purpose of further investigation.
💡 Memory Tip
Hypo = under, thesis = idea. A hypothesis sits "under" proof — it's an idea waiting to be supported.
Ecosystem
noun
📖 Definition
A biological community of interacting organisms and their physical environment, functioning together as a unit.
✏️ Example
"The removal of apex predators can destabilise an entire ecosystem through a cascading series of ecological effects."
🔗 Collocations
fragile ecosystem restore an ecosystem disrupt the ecosystem
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"Upset the balance" — to disturb the natural equilibrium of an ecosystem, often with far-reaching consequences.
💡 Memory Tip
Eco (home/environment) + system (organised whole). Picture a house where every resident — plant, animal, microbe — plays a role.
Catalyst
noun
📖 Definition
A substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself being consumed; more broadly, any agent that accelerates change.
✏️ Example
"Enzymes act as biological catalysts, dramatically speeding up metabolic reactions that would otherwise occur too slowly to sustain life."
🔗 Collocations
act as a catalyst serve as a catalyst for biological catalyst
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"The spark that ignites" — a catalyst is the spark that sets a process in motion without being burned up itself.
💡 Memory Tip
Think of a catalyst as a matchstick: it lights the fire but doesn't become the fire. It enables without being used up.
Metamorphosis
noun
📖 Definition
A process of transformation from an immature form to an adult form in two or more distinct stages; broadly, a profound change in form or nature.
✏️ Example
"The metamorphosis of a caterpillar into a butterfly is one of the most dramatic examples of biological transformation in the natural world."
🔗 Collocations
undergo metamorphosis complete metamorphosis remarkable metamorphosis
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"A sea change" — a profound transformation, echoing the total reinvention seen in metamorphosis.
💡 Memory Tip
Meta (change) + morph (form). Morphing superheroes change shape — metamorphosis is nature's version of that shape-shift.
Symbiosis
noun
📖 Definition
A close, long-term interaction between two different biological species, which may be mutually beneficial, neutral, or harmful to one party.
✏️ Example
"The relationship between clownfish and sea anemones is a classic example of mutualistic symbiosis, with each species benefiting from the other's presence."
🔗 Collocations
mutualistic symbiosis live in symbiosis ecological symbiosis
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"Scratch my back and I'll scratch yours" — captures the mutual-benefit essence of symbiotic relationships in plain language.
💡 Memory Tip
Sym (together) + bios (life). Two lives living together — picture a tiny pilot fish riding a shark, cleaning it in return for scraps.
Volatile
adjective
📖 Definition
In science, readily evaporating at normal temperatures; more broadly, liable to change rapidly and unpredictably, especially for the worse.
✏️ Example
"Certain volatile organic compounds released by plants serve as chemical signals that attract pollinators or repel herbivores."
🔗 Collocations
volatile compounds highly volatile volatile substance
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"Sitting on a powder keg" — describes an inherently volatile situation that could ignite at any moment, just as volatile chemicals can combust.
💡 Memory Tip
Think of volcano: volatile and volcano share the Latin root volare (to fly). Volatile substances fly off — they evaporate quickly and unpredictably.
