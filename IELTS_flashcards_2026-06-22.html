IELTS Vocabulary Flashcards

Monday, 22 June 2026  ·  8 words

deforestation
noun
Definition
The large-scale removal or destruction of forests, typically to clear land for agriculture, urban development, or logging.
Example
Rampant deforestation in tropical regions has accelerated species extinction and contributed significantly to rising global temperatures.
Collocations
rampant deforestation combat deforestation deforestation rate
Idiom / Phrase
can't see the wood for the trees — so focused on detail that the bigger environmental picture (like deforestation's global impact) is missed.
Memory Tip
De-forest-ation — picture "de-" (removing) + "forest" disappearing. The forest is being un-forested.
biodiversity
noun
Definition
The variety of plant, animal, and microorganism species within a given habitat or on Earth as a whole, often used as a measure of ecosystem health.
Example
Scientists warn that declining biodiversity in coral reef ecosystems undermines their resilience to ocean warming and acidification.
Collocations
rich biodiversity biodiversity loss protect biodiversity
Idiom / Phrase
web of life — biodiversity is the intricate web connecting all living things; remove one thread and the whole structure weakens.
Memory Tip
Bio (life) + diversity (variety). Think of a vast, colourful market stall — life in all its variety.
ecosystem
noun
Definition
A biological community of interacting organisms and their physical environment, functioning together as a system.
Example
The introduction of invasive species can disrupt a fragile ecosystem, displacing native fauna and altering nutrient cycles.
Collocations
fragile ecosystem ecosystem services disrupt an ecosystem
Idiom / Phrase
the balance of nature — an ecosystem thrives when each organism plays its role; tip that balance and the whole system suffers.
Memory Tip
Eco (home/environment) + system (working together). Think of a house where every room and resident depend on each other.
greenhouse gas
noun phrase
Definition
Any gas in the atmosphere — such as carbon dioxide, methane, or nitrous oxide — that absorbs and re-emits infrared radiation, trapping heat and warming the planet.
Example
International agreements aim to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by transitioning economies away from coal and oil dependency.
Collocations
greenhouse gas emissions cut greenhouse gases greenhouse effect
Idiom / Phrase
turn up the heat — literally what greenhouse gases do to the planet; figuratively, to intensify pressure on a situation.
Memory Tip
Imagine the Earth wrapped in a glass greenhouse: gases act like the glass panes, letting sunlight in but trapping warmth inside.
renewable energy
noun phrase
Definition
Energy derived from naturally replenishing sources — such as sunlight, wind, water, and geothermal heat — that do not deplete over time.
Example
Governments worldwide are investing heavily in renewable energy infrastructure to reduce dependence on finite fossil fuels.
Collocations
renewable energy source invest in renewables renewable capacity
Idiom / Phrase
harnessing the wind — to capture and use a powerful, natural force productively, just as wind turbines do.
Memory Tip
Renewable = can be renewed. Sun rises every morning, wind never stops — nature keeps recharging the supply.
carbon footprint
noun phrase
Definition
The total amount of greenhouse gases — measured in CO₂ equivalents — produced directly or indirectly by an individual, organisation, or product.
Example
Airlines are under pressure to shrink their carbon footprint by investing in sustainable aviation fuels and more efficient aircraft.
Collocations
reduce/shrink carbon footprint carbon footprint calculator offset a carbon footprint
Idiom / Phrase
leave a light footprint — to travel or live with minimal environmental impact, treading gently on the planet.
Memory Tip
Picture dirty carbon-black footprints you stamp wherever you go. A bigger lifestyle = bigger, blacker prints left behind.
sustainable
adjective
Definition
Able to be maintained over the long term without depleting natural resources or causing irreversible environmental harm.
Example
Adopting sustainable agricultural practices — such as crop rotation and reduced pesticide use — helps preserve soil fertility for future generations.
Collocations
sustainable development sustainable agriculture environmentally sustainable
Idiom / Phrase
think long-term — sustainability is about planning beyond immediate gain; looking decades ahead rather than the next quarter.
Memory Tip
Sustain = to keep going. Sustainable = something you can keep doing indefinitely without using it up — like a steady river, not a flash flood.
conservation
noun
Definition
The careful management and protection of natural environments, wildlife, and resources to prevent their degradation or extinction.
Example
Effective wildlife conservation requires not only protected reserves but also community engagement and sustainable livelihood alternatives for local populations.
Collocations
wildlife conservation conservation effort energy conservation
Idiom / Phrase
preserve for posterity — to keep something intact so future generations can experience and benefit from it, the core mission of conservation.
Memory Tip
Conserve = to keep safe. Think of a museum conserving precious artefacts — nature conservationists do the same for living things.
