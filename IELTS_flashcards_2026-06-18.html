pedagogy
noun
Definition

The theory and practice of teaching; the methods and principles that guide educational instruction.

Example

"Effective pedagogy requires teachers to adapt their methods to the diverse needs of every learner in the classroom."

Collocations

critical pedagogy · progressive pedagogy · culturally responsive pedagogy

Idiom / Phrase

the art of teaching — pedagogy is often described as both a science and an art, blending theory with craft.

Memory Tip

"Peda-" → pied (Latin for foot) → a teacher leads pupils step by step on foot.

curriculum
noun
Definition

The structured set of subjects, courses, and learning experiences that a school or institution prescribes for students.

Example

"The national curriculum was revised to place greater emphasis on digital literacy and critical thinking skills."

Collocations

national curriculum · hidden curriculum · curriculum design

Idiom / Phrase

on the curriculum — if a subject is "on the curriculum," it is officially taught; if not, it is deemed outside the scope.

Memory Tip

Latin currere = to run. Picture students running a race course laid out by the school.

assessment
noun
Definition

The process of evaluating a student's knowledge, skills, or performance, typically through tests, assignments, or observations.

Example

"Continuous assessment throughout the semester provides a more accurate picture of student progress than a single final examination."

Collocations

formative assessment · summative assessment · peer assessment

Idiom / Phrase

take stock of — assessment is essentially "taking stock" of what a learner knows before moving forward.

Memory Tip

"Assess" → sit beside (Latin assidere) → an assessor sits beside you to judge your work.

literacy
noun
Definition

The ability to read and write; more broadly, competence in a specific area of knowledge or skill.

Example

"Improving adult literacy rates in rural communities remains a key priority for the government's education reform agenda."

Collocations

digital literacy · financial literacy · literacy rate

Idiom / Phrase

read between the lines — true literacy means understanding not just what is written, but what is implied.

Memory Tip

"Litera" = letter in Latin. Literacy = mastery of the letters that form words.

cognition
noun
Definition

The mental processes involved in acquiring knowledge, including perception, reasoning, memory, and problem-solving.

Example

"Research suggests that physical exercise positively influences cognition by increasing blood flow to the brain."

Collocations

higher-order cognition · social cognition · cognitive development

Idiom / Phrase

food for thought — anything that stimulates cognition by prompting deeper mental processing.

Memory Tip

Latin cognoscere = to know. "Cogito, ergo sum" — Descartes' famous phrase on the thinking mind.

metacognition
noun
Definition

Thinking about one's own thinking; the awareness and regulation of one's cognitive processes while learning.

Example

"Students who practise metacognition are better able to identify gaps in their understanding and adjust their study strategies accordingly."

Collocations

metacognitive strategies · metacognitive awareness · promote metacognition

Idiom / Phrase

know thyself — the ancient Greek maxim captures the essence of metacognition: understanding your own mind.

Memory Tip

"Meta-" = beyond/about. Metacognition = thinking about (beyond) your cognition — your brain watching itself think.

scaffolding
noun
Definition

Temporary support provided by a teacher or more skilled peer to help a learner accomplish a task they could not yet manage independently.

Example

"The teacher used scaffolding techniques such as graphic organisers and worked examples to help students tackle complex essay writing."

Collocations

instructional scaffolding · provide scaffolding · gradually remove scaffolding

Idiom / Phrase

training wheels — scaffolding works like training wheels: helpful initially, removed once the learner can balance alone.

Memory Tip

Picture construction scaffolding on a building — it supports the structure while it's being built, then comes down.

differentiation
noun
Definition

The practice of tailoring teaching methods, materials, and tasks to accommodate the varying abilities and learning styles of individual students.

Example

"Effective differentiation enables both gifted learners and those with additional needs to make meaningful progress within the same classroom."

Collocations

classroom differentiation · differentiate instruction · differentiated learning

Idiom / Phrase

one size does not fit all — differentiation is the direct pedagogical response to this reality.

Memory Tip

"Differ" = be different. Differentiation = making differences in your teaching to match differences in your learners.
