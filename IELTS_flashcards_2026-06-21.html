IELTS Vocabulary: Science & Nature

8 academic words to master today · flip, read, and repeat

ecosystem noun
Definition
A complex network of living organisms, their physical environment, and the interactions between them functioning as a unit.
Example
The destruction of the Amazon rainforest threatens one of the most diverse ecosystems on Earth.
Collocations
fragile ecosystem · marine ecosystem · restore an ecosystem
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
a delicate balance — used to describe how easily an ecosystem can be disrupted: "Removing one predator upsets the delicate balance of the ecosystem."
Memory Tip
Think "ECO" (environment) + "SYSTEM" — it's a living system plugged into the environment.
1 / 8
biodiversity noun
Definition
The variety of plant and animal life in a particular habitat or on Earth as a whole, considered to be important for ecological stability.
Example
Scientists warn that current rates of species extinction pose a severe threat to global biodiversity.
Collocations
rich biodiversity · preserve biodiversity · loss of biodiversity
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
a web of life — captures how biodiversity links every species: "Biodiversity is the web of life that sustains all living things."
Memory Tip
BIO (life) + DIVERSITY (variety) = the variety of life. Think of a rainforest bursting with different creatures.
2 / 8
photosynthesis noun
Definition
The process by which green plants and algae use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to produce oxygen and glucose for energy.
Example
Without photosynthesis, the entire food chain that sustains life on Earth would collapse.
Collocations
rate of photosynthesis · undergo photosynthesis · photosynthetic organisms
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
harnessing the sun — often used in essays: "Plants harness the sun through photosynthesis to power all life on Earth."
Memory Tip
PHOTO (light) + SYNTHESIS (making) = plants making food from light. Picture a leaf "eating" sunlight.
3 / 8
hypothesis noun
Definition
A proposed explanation for an observed phenomenon, put forward as a starting point for further investigation and testing.
Example
The researcher formulated a hypothesis that increased UV exposure accelerates the aging of skin cells.
Collocations
formulate a hypothesis · test a hypothesis · support / refute a hypothesis
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
put to the test — "Every hypothesis must eventually be put to the test through rigorous experimentation."
Memory Tip
HYPO (under/below) — it's a theory "beneath" proof, waiting to be confirmed. An educated guess on a ladder.
4 / 8
symbiosis noun
Definition
A close, long-term interaction between two different species, often but not always to the mutual benefit of both organisms.
Example
The symbiosis between bees and flowering plants is essential for pollination and food production worldwide.
Collocations
mutually beneficial symbiosis · symbiotic relationship · enter into symbiosis
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
scratch each other's backs — informal equivalent for mutual symbiosis: "The clownfish and sea anemone effectively scratch each other's backs."
Memory Tip
SYM (together) + BIO (life) = living together. Imagine two creatures sharing a cozy apartment.
5 / 8
adaptation noun
Definition
A physical or behavioural characteristic that has developed over time and increases an organism's ability to survive in its environment.
Example
The polar bear's thick layer of insulating fat is a remarkable adaptation to Arctic conditions.
Collocations
evolutionary adaptation · remarkable adaptation · adapt to the environment
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
survival of the fittest — adaptations drive this: "Only organisms with the most effective adaptations survive — survival of the fittest."
Memory Tip
ADAPT = to fit. An adaptation is nature's custom-fit suit for surviving a harsh environment.
6 / 8
mutation noun
Definition
A permanent alteration in the DNA sequence of a gene, which may result in a change in the characteristics of an organism.
Example
Certain genetic mutations increase an individual's susceptibility to hereditary forms of cancer.
Collocations
genetic mutation · random mutation · undergo a mutation
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
a double-edged sword — mutations can help or harm: "Mutations are a double-edged sword — driving evolution but also disease."
Memory Tip
MUTATE = change. Think of a typo in a recipe — sometimes the dish is ruined, sometimes it's better!
7 / 8
osmosis noun
Definition
The process by which molecules of a solvent pass through a semipermeable membrane from a less concentrated solution to a more concentrated one.
Example
Plant roots absorb water from the soil primarily through osmosis, driven by differences in solute concentration.
Collocations
reverse osmosis · osmotic pressure · process of osmosis
Idiom / Fixed Phrase
by osmosis (figurative) — absorbing knowledge passively: "She seemed to learn new languages by osmosis, picking them up effortlessly."
Memory Tip
Picture water "sneaking" through a membrane wall from a watery side to a salty side — it always moves toward the thirstier side.
