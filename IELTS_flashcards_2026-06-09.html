IELTS Vocabulary

Health & Medicine

Pandemic
noun
📖 Definition
An outbreak of a disease that spreads across multiple countries or the entire world, affecting a large number of people simultaneously.
✏️ Example
"The 2020 pandemic exposed severe inadequacies in global healthcare infrastructure and emergency preparedness systems."
🔗 Collocations
global pandemic contain a pandemic pandemic response
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"flatten the curve" — to slow the spread of disease so hospitals are not overwhelmed.
💡 Memory Tip
Pan (Greek: all) + demos (people) → a disease that reaches all people.
Prognosis
noun
📖 Definition
A medical forecast of the likely outcome or course of a disease, including the chance of recovery.
✏️ Example
"Early diagnosis significantly improves the prognosis for patients with certain forms of cancer."
🔗 Collocations
poor prognosis favourable prognosis long-term prognosis
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"the outlook is grim" — used when the prognosis for a patient or situation is very serious.
💡 Memory Tip
Pro (before) + gnosis (knowledge) → knowing beforehand what will happen. Like a medical crystal ball.
Alleviate
verb
📖 Definition
To make pain, suffering, or a problem less severe or intense; to provide partial relief without completely eliminating the issue.
✏️ Example
"Palliative care is designed to alleviate the suffering of terminally ill patients rather than cure the underlying condition."
🔗 Collocations
alleviate pain alleviate symptoms alleviate pressure
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"take the edge off" — to partially reduce pain or discomfort, similar to alleviating it slightly.
💡 Memory Tip
Think of a-LEVIATE → to lift (levitate) the burden of pain off someone.
Chronic
adjective
📖 Definition
Describing a condition or disease that persists for a long time (typically more than three months) or recurs frequently over time.
✏️ Example
"Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease places enormous strain on healthcare systems in industrialised nations."
🔗 Collocations
chronic illness chronic pain chronic shortage
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"a lingering problem" — something that stays around persistently, just like a chronic condition.
💡 Memory Tip
Chron = time (chronology, chronicle) → chronic = something stuck in time, never leaving.
Prevalent
adjective
📖 Definition
Widespread; commonly occurring or existing in a particular area, group, or population at a given time.
✏️ Example
"Obesity is becoming increasingly prevalent in developing countries as sedentary lifestyles and processed food consumption rise."
🔗 Collocations
increasingly prevalent prevalent among most prevalent disease
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"rife with" — used to mean something is very prevalent or widespread: "The region is rife with infectious disease."
💡 Memory Tip
Prevail + -ent → what prevails (wins out, dominates) in a population is prevalent.
Immunisation
noun
📖 Definition
The process of making a person or animal immune to a disease, typically through vaccination, so the immune system can recognise and fight future infections.
✏️ Example
"Widespread immunisation programmes have been credited with the near-eradication of polio in most parts of the world."
🔗 Collocations
immunisation programme childhood immunisation mass immunisation
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"herd immunity" — when enough of a population is immunised that disease spread is suppressed even for those not vaccinated.
💡 Memory Tip
Think immune + -isation → the action of making immune. A shield-building process.
Sedentary
adjective
📖 Definition
Characterised by much sitting or little physical activity; a lifestyle or occupation that involves minimal movement.
✏️ Example
"Health authorities warn that sedentary behaviour among office workers significantly increases the risk of cardiovascular disease."
🔗 Collocations
sedentary lifestyle sedentary behaviour sedentary occupation
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"couch potato" — informal expression for someone with an extremely sedentary lifestyle who spends most time sitting.
💡 Memory Tip
Sedens (Latin: sitting) → sedentary = someone who is always seated. A sediment also just sits at the bottom.
Pathogen
noun
📖 Definition
A microorganism — such as a bacterium, virus, fungus, or parasite — that causes disease in its host.
✏️ Example
"Researchers are developing novel antiviral compounds capable of neutralising previously untreatable pathogens."
🔗 Collocations
airborne pathogen deadly pathogen transmit a pathogen
💬 Idiom / Phrase
"patient zero" — the first person infected by a pathogen in an outbreak, from whom transmission begins.
💡 Memory Tip
Pathos (suffering) + gen (generates) → a pathogen generates suffering. The villain of microbiology.
