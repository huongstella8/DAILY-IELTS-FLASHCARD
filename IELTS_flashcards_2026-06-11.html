IELTS Vocabulary

Education & Learning

Thursday, June 11, 2026  ·  8 flashcards

pedagogy noun
Definition The theory and practice of teaching; the methods and principles used to instruct learners effectively.
Example Reforms in higher education have increasingly focused on student-centred pedagogy rather than traditional lecture-based instruction.
Collocations
  • student-centred pedagogy
  • progressive pedagogy
  • adopt a new pedagogy
Idiom / Fixed Phrase "chalk and talk" — a traditional pedagogical style relying solely on a teacher talking and writing on the board, now often criticised as outdated.
Memory Tip Peda- comes from Greek pais (child) + agogos (leader) — the one who leads the child through learning.
curriculum noun
Definition The subjects and content comprising a course of study in a school, college, or university; the overall plan of educational experiences.
Example Critics argue that the national curriculum places excessive emphasis on rote memorisation at the expense of critical thinking skills.
Collocations
  • national curriculum
  • revise / overhaul the curriculum
  • curriculum design
Idiom / Fixed Phrase "hidden curriculum" — the unspoken social norms and values students implicitly absorb in school beyond the formal syllabus.
Memory Tip Latin for "racecourse" — think of the curriculum as the track students run through during their schooling.
cognitive adjective
Definition Relating to the mental processes of acquiring knowledge and understanding through thought, experience, and the senses.
Example Bilingual education has been shown to enhance cognitive flexibility and improve problem-solving abilities in young learners.
Collocations
  • cognitive development
  • cognitive skills / abilities
  • cognitive load
Idiom / Fixed Phrase "cognitive overload" — the state of having too much information to process at once, hindering effective learning.
Memory Tip Think cogito — Descartes' "I think, therefore I am." Cognitive = anything to do with thinking.
scholarship noun
Definition (1) A grant of financial aid awarded to a student based on academic merit or need; (2) serious academic study and the knowledge derived from it.
Example Governments should expand scholarship programmes to ensure that talented students from disadvantaged backgrounds can access higher education.
Collocations
  • award / receive a scholarship
  • academic scholarship
  • scholarly research
Idiom / Fixed Phrase "a man/woman of letters" — a person deeply devoted to scholarly learning and literature, sharing the spirit of scholarship.
Memory Tip A scholar + ship = a vessel carrying knowledge. The scholarship sails you toward learning.
facilitate verb
Definition To make a process or activity easier or more achievable; in education, to guide and support learning rather than simply transmit information.
Example Technology can facilitate collaborative learning by enabling students in different countries to work together on shared projects.
Collocations
  • facilitate learning / discussion
  • facilitate access to education
  • facilitate collaboration
Idiom / Fixed Phrase "pave the way" — to facilitate future progress by removing obstacles, often used when describing educational reforms.
Memory Tip Comes from Latin facilis (easy). To facilitate = to make things facile (easy).
assimilate verb
Definition To absorb and integrate new information, ideas, or people into an existing framework of knowledge or society.
Example Students who read widely are better equipped to assimilate complex academic concepts and apply them across different disciplines.
Collocations
  • assimilate information / knowledge
  • assimilate into a culture
  • rapidly assimilate
Idiom / Fixed Phrase "take on board" — informal equivalent of assimilate; to accept and incorporate new information or feedback.
Memory Tip Think of a sponge (similis = similar) — it absorbs and becomes similar to what it soaks up.
vocational adjective
Definition Relating to education or training that prepares students for a specific trade, craft, or career rather than academic study.
Example Many economists argue that greater investment in vocational training would address skill shortages more effectively than expanding university places.
Collocations
  • vocational training / education
  • vocational qualifications
  • vocational skills
Idiom / Fixed Phrase "learn the ropes" — to learn the practical skills of a trade or job, aligned closely with the spirit of vocational training.
Memory Tip From Latin vocare (to call) — a vocation is a "calling" to a specific trade; vocational trains you for it.
autonomy noun
Definition The capacity and freedom to make one's own decisions; in education, a learner's ability to direct and take responsibility for their own learning.
Example Fostering learner autonomy is a central goal of modern education, equipping students with the self-management skills needed for lifelong learning.
Collocations
  • learner autonomy
  • promote / foster autonomy
  • institutional autonomy
Idiom / Fixed Phrase "stand on one's own two feet" — to act independently without relying on others, reflecting the essence of learner autonomy.
Memory Tip Auto (self) + nomos (law) = making your own rules. An autonomous learner is their own teacher.
